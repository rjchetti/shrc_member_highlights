# Member Guide: Sharing Your Highlights

As a member of the Seoul Hanmaum Rotary Club, your professional achievements inspire others and strengthen our club's reputation. This guide explains how to use the Highlight System.

## 1. Authentication
*   Visit the [Member Highlights Web App](#).
*   Click **"Sign in with Google"** or use your Email.
*   *Note: Only active club members should submit highlights.*

## 2. Filling out the Form
The form is bilingual. You can toggle between **English (EN)** and **Korean (KO)** at the top right.

### Required Fields:
*   **Full Name**: Your name as you'd like it to appear in the newsletter.
*   **Club Name**: Select "Seoul Hanmaum" or your specific satellite club.
*   **Professional Title**: Your current designation (e.g., Executive Director, Founder, Surgeon).
*   **Company/Organization**: The name of your workplace.
*   **Highlight Title**: A short, catchy title for your update (e.g., "Elected as District Governor").
*   **Highlight Body**: Describe your achievement. You can use the **Bold** and **Link** buttons in the editor to emphasize text or link to a website/article.
*   **Photo**: Upload a high-resolution professional headshot. 

## 3. Writing in the 3rd Person
To help our PR Chair publish your highlight faster, we encourage you to write in the **3rd Person POV**.
*   **Correct**: "Jane Doe was recently honored with the Service Above Self award for her work in..."
*   **Avoid**: "I was recently honored with..."

## 4. Editing Your Submission
*   You can only have **one active highlight** per month.
*   If you've already submitted one, logging back in will show your current entry.
*   Simply update the fields and click **"Save Changes"** to overwrite the previous entry.

## 4. Best Practices
> [!TIP]
> **Photos**: Use a photo with a simple background. The system will automatically crop it into a professional format for the newsletter.
>
> **Language**: If you can, provide your highlight in both languages by toggling the switch and entering the translated text. If not, the PR Chair will handle the translation for you.
